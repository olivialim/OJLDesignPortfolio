S L A N T N U

Work with team of seven at Northwestern University

SLANT emerged from a conversation among five friends at Northwestern who wanted to try a social experiment, frustrated with available traditional publications. To fill this void, SLANT offers a dynamic, collaborative, feel-good space for student-artists to exchange and create ideas by publishing quarterly themed issues to present their creative projects. We, as a collective, strive to push the boundaries of available resources in order to showcase pieces in a provcative way.


Categories :Web Development, UI/UX Design, Graphic Design

Number of Issues :Seven

See on SLANT NU

As editor-in-chief and co-founder of SLANT NU, I worked with other students to create a platform for students who want to present their multimedia pieces. Discovering this space has allowed for I had the fortunate opportunity to develop the publication Working with in a group of four at the Flatiron School, I was able to brainstorm and ideate, while creating efficient and powerful code. For three weeks, we went through daily code reviews with instructors and incorporated various technologies, for both learning and experience sake.


In the seven issues launched, I contributed with the following:
  • led a team of seven to workshop and refine pieces with contributors we recruit
  • designed and created seven websites for the online publication for visually dynamic presentations
  • created a social media presence through Facebook (~450 likes) and Instagram (~150 followers)
  • generated 700+ unique visitors in the first week of launching of each issue
